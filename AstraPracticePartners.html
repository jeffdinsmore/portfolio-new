PROJECT SUMMARY

Moda Health built a new website for subsidiary Astra Practice Partners to enable dental practices to purchase goods and services.

I was tasked to build the site map, design and prototype the navigation, and make the site mobile responsive.

Because the products offered were diverse, showcasing them in the navigation created a challenging user experience.

The final design included a streamlined display of product options and a mobile responsive site with uncomplicated navigation.

Overview

Project: Website for Moda Health Partner

Role: UX Designer

Skills: Human-centered design, user flow, journey mapping, wireframing, iterative prototyping

Tools: Axure RP, Sketch

Duration: Three months

CHALLENGE & GOAL

Challenge

The many diverse products offered by the company made it difficult to organize and display them in the navigation.

The team's original implementation of a mega menu created a confusing user experience.

Goal

  • Design a convenient navigation bar.
  • Make the website mobile responsive.
  • Offer goods and services with user-friendly corresponding drop down menus.

Design


Utilizing iterative design, I created the strongest navigation bar option.

First test: When users clicked menu items, the drop down menu would populate. The item needed to be clicked again to collapse the drop down menu.

Second test: A flyout drop down menu. When the cursor hovered over the menu item, the drop down appeared. After the cursor left the menu item, the drop down would disappear.

First iteration of the mobile navigation.
First Iteration Desktop

How much content do we want in the drop down menu? Do we want a flyout menu? Or a click to open and click to close menu?

Third test: A partial flyout drop down menu. When a menu item was clicked, the drop down appeared. When the cursor left the menu item, the drop down disappeared.

Second iteration of the mobile nav.
Second Iteration Desktop

Cleaner design, but too much content overwhelmed the user.

Solution: User research illustrated a strong preference to click a menu item to expand the drop down menu, then click anywhere on the page to collapse the drop down menu.

The same approach was used for the drop down menus in the hamburger nav.

The final design on the mobile view eliminated multiple drop downs for simplification.
Final Desktop Design

Condensed content to drive users to explore more products.

CONCLUSION

The greatest challenge was to condense and simplify information in the drop down menus.

The complexity of the diverse products drove the decision to list general equipment categories in the drop down menu which leads users to subpages that include devices and their details.

The original flyout menu received negative feedback because clients preferred the drop down menu to stay open. Implementing a drop down menu that remained expanded after clicking a menu item provided a positive user experience.

The final design resulted in a streamlined display of products and a mobile responsive site with an uncomplicated mobile navigation.
